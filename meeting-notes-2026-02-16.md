## SESSION 3: Striker Analysis — Separation & Movement

### Match: Eastleigh vs Borough 6-0 Hampton & Richmond (29/11/25)

#### Topic: Separation on Crosses (Tactical Graphic Shown)
Flavius showed a detailed graphic on **"Separation on Crosses"** with key visuals:
- **Bad body shape:** Arms not outstretched, defender is all over him, able to get very close
- **Vertical of centre of gravity** — important concept for strikers
- **Poor technique:** Body squares to the defender → defender able to poke ball away, also can be unbalanced fairly easily
- **Foul drawing:** Shirt pull situations
- **Good technique:** Uses penalty spot as reference point for positioning

#### Topic: Receiving the Ball & Creating Space
- Good striker creates separation DURING a duel
- Receives ball without pressure — needs 1.5 to 2 meters of space
- Controls, then takes the defender on
- **"Makes a really good striker. Something that we are definitely looking for"**

#### Topic: Movement Patterns — Double Movement
- Striker is NOT going towards the ball — he runs OPPOSITE direction
- Uses the penalty spot as a reference point
- Finishes off the chance after creating space
- **"Double movement"** — go towards ball then change direction to gain separation from defender
- Can also be called a "step" movement
- **"If a player does that consistently — super important"** — note this in reports

#### Topic: Carl (Liga II Striker) — Negative Example
- Carl was "the go-getter" of the championship, fully devoted
- But even being very good for Liga II, he was **unable to get separation in the box**
- Lacked the **instinct to change direction and confuse the defender**
- **Key lesson:** "No striker is ever going to have everything, but we just need to know exactly what he can do and what he can't do"
- Goal is to make decisions with as much knowledge about the player's profile as possible

## SESSION 4: What Flavius Wants Scouts to Look For

### Striker Evaluation Criteria (from this meeting):
1. **Separation ability** — Can the striker create space from defenders in the box?
2. **Double movement** — Does he use feints/direction changes to lose markers?
3. **Body shape when receiving** — Arms out, centre of gravity, not squared to defender
4. **Penalty spot awareness** — Uses reference points for positioning
5. **Receiving under pressure** — Can he control with 1.5-2m of space?
6. **Instinct to confuse** — Direction changes, not just running at defender
7. **Consistency** — Does he do these things regularly or just occasionally?

### DM Evaluation Criteria (from this meeting):
1. **Screening** — Positioning between ball and goal
2. **Anticipation** — Reading play before it develops
3. **Body shape** — Correct shape to cut passing lanes
4. **Ball watching** — Mental lapses, tuning out
5. **Pressing commitment** — Decisive, no hesitation
6. **Long ball coverage** — Protecting channels
